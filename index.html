Hey there Love,
I hope you like flowers,
And words that bloom like they do.
On this day, our day, a year past,
I pen these lines, my heart in every word.

Ever since I asked you out on this day, January 16,
I've known happiness in its purest form.
You, my love, are the sweetest song,
The melody in the morning's dew,
The gentle whisper in the night's hush.

Each day, you're the sun's first light,
And the moon's last sigh.
You're the sweetest girl I've ever met,
And I can't help but think about you each day.

I apologize, my dear, for this letter,
For words may seem a poor substitute for presence.
But know that it's not a celebration over others' misery,
Rather, a quiet testament of our love's resiliency.

We've had our ups and downs,
But I'm glad that we lasted through it all.
I miss you, like the desert misses the rain,
Like the night sky misses the day's vibrant stain.

And in this longing, I find a song,
A melody, a rhythm, where my heart belongs.
So here's my song, my love, my life,
A symphony of words, cutting through strife.

I love you, more than words can say,
Today, tomorrow, and every single day.
